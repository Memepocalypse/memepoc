Introduction to Memes

Memes have been an integral part of human expression for centuries. This document explores their evolution from ancient cultural symbols to digital crypto assets.

Timeline of Memes

Ancient Roman Graffiti (79 CE, Pompeii)

The eruption of Mt. Vesuvius preserved Roman graffiti that mocked politicians and public figures.

Ancient Egyptian Hieroglyphs (c. 3200 BCE)

Satirical hieroglyphs show animals performing human tasks, representing early humor in ancient Egypt.

Kilroy Was Here (1940s, WWII)

This iconic meme spread globally among WWII soldiers, showing that memes could go viral even without the internet.

Dogecoin and Crypto Memes (2013)

Dogecoin was created as a joke but became a widely popular cryptocurrency due to its meme-based community.

SPX6900 Meme Coin (2024)

SPX6900 emerged as a humorous yet technologically advanced meme coin during the 2024 meme coin surge.

Conclusion

From hieroglyphs to blockchain, memes have shown adaptability, influencing finance and culture. Memepocalypse (MEMEPOC) celebrates this journey, bridging ancient humor with modern innovation.
